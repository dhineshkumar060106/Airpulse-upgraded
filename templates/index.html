Risk Assessment

Personalized health recommendations based on AQI levels and pollutant profiles.

AQI 0–50
🟢 Good
Air quality is satisfactory. Little or no risk.
✅ All outdoor activities safe
✅ Normal exercise levels
✅ Windows can be open
AQI 51–100
🟡 Moderate
Unusually sensitive individuals may experience mild symptoms.
⚠️ Sensitive groups limit prolonged exertion
✅ Most activities remain safe
⚠️ Monitor for unusual symptoms
AQI 101–150
🟠 Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
People with asthma, heart disease at risk. General public less affected.
🚫 Children & elderly limit outdoor time
😷 Consider N95 mask outdoors
🏠 Use air purifier indoors
AQI 151–200
🔴 Unhealthy
Everyone may experience health effects. Sensitive groups severe effects.
🚫 Avoid heavy outdoor exertion
😷 Wear N95 mask when outdoors
💊 Keep rescue inhaler handy
🏠 Close windows, use AC/purifier
AQI 201–300
🟣 Very Unhealthy
Health alert: serious risk for everyone.
🚫 Avoid all outdoor activities
😷 N95 mandatory if going out
🏥 Seek medical attention if symptomatic
💧 Stay hydrated, use air purifier
AQI 300+
☠️ Hazardous
Emergency conditions. Entire population at serious risk.
🚨 Stay indoors — seal windows & doors
🚫 No outdoor activities whatsoever
🏥 Emergency medical preparedness
📺 Follow official advisories
